# Troop conversion v2 — published-grammar strike conversion

**Audience:** a CRISPR session with no other context. Everything needed is restated here.
**Requested by:** Mark, 2026-07-18, after a full review of the published troop corpus. Decisions
marked "(Mark)" were ruled in that discussion.
**Execution mode:** autonomous, wave-by-wave, via the agent/model matrix in §Execution protocol.
**Builds on:** `docs/plans/troop-build-api-for-reignmaker.md` (W1–W5 shipped, commits
f85b396→029c1a7). That plan made troop-ness structurally correct (trait, seed-if-missing
weaknesses, glossary kit, export). This plan makes the *conversion* produce a real troop
statblock: strikes become the published attack actions, generated by CRISPR itself instead of a
consumer recipe.

## Why this exists

`applyTroopConversion` currently delegates all offense generation to a provider's
`TroopConversionRecipe.generateAbilities`. The only implementation (RM's `armyAbilityProvider`)
diverges from the published grammar on every axis: three separate generic items ("Troop Attack
One/Two/Three") instead of the canonical single 1-to-3-action scaler, reads `strikes[0]` only
(ranged strikes never become a volley), no `@Template`, no `options:area-damage`/`area-effect`
automation tags, DC ~3 too high, damage math unrelated to published progression, strikes never
removed, and a hand-authored Troop Defenses that duplicates the glossary item. A recipe-less
conversion does almost nothing. CRISPR is the creature tool; the engine moves upstream (Mark's
standing ruling), and the recipe becomes an override layer.

## Verified corpus facts (drove every decision)

Sweep of all 162 troop-trait NPCs in `_pf2e-source` `packs/pf2e/**` plus 20 verified
troop↔base-creature pairs, 2026-07-18. The numbers below are the durable record.

1. **Troop level = base level + 5** (15/20 pairs; +6 twice; "veteran/elite" formations +8..+12).
2. **No published troop has a melee/ranged strike item — zero of 162.** All offense is `action`
   items with inline `@Damage`/`@Check`.
3. **The canonical attack is ONE 1-to-3-action item** (151/162): `actions.value: 1`, glyph header
   "1 to 3", usually **Frequency once per round**, effect "each enemy in a
   `@Template[type:emanation|distance:5]`" (10 ft for reach weapons), save
   `@Check[reflex|dc:N|basic|options:area-effect]` — **all 159 such items use Reflex** — then three
   damage lines tagged `|options:area-damage`. Exact markup (Hobgoblin Veteran Regiment):

   ```html
   <p><span class="action-glyph">1</span> to <span class="action-glyph">3</span></p>
   <p><strong>Frequency</strong> once per round</p><hr />
   <p><strong>Effect</strong> The hobgoblins engage in a coordinated melee attack against each
   enemy in a @Template[type:emanation|distance:5], with a
   @Check[reflex|dc:25|basic|options:area-effect] save. The damage depends on the number of
   actions.</p>
   <p><span class="action-glyph">1</span> @Damage[(1d8+2)[slashing]|options:area-damage] damage</p>
   <p><span class="action-glyph">2</span> @Damage[(2d8+9)[slashing]|options:area-damage] damage</p>
   <p><span class="action-glyph">3</span> @Damage[(3d8+11)[slashing]|options:area-damage] damage</p>
   ```

4. **Sweep damage keeps the base strike's die size** (Wolf d6 jaws → 1d6+1 / 2d6+7 / 2d6+11;
   Viking d8 axes → 1d8+3 / 2d8+12 / 3d8+15). Pattern: N dice → 2N dice + large mod → 3N dice +
   (mod+3), **d4 capped at 4 dice** (then only the mod grows). Secondary damage components
   (hell hound fire, omox acid) ride along and escalate slower (1 die → 2 dice). Published
   per-level median averages of the three lines:

   | Lvl | 1a | 2a | 3a | | Lvl | 1a | 2a | 3a |
   |-----|-----|------|------|-|-----|------|------|------|
   | 2 | 5.5 | 8.5 | 11.5 | | 10 | 6.5 | 20 | 26.5 |
   | 3 | 3.5 | 9 | 13 | | 11 | 8.5 | 21 | 28.5 |
   | 4 | 3.5 | 11 | 14 | | 12 | 7 | 22.5 | 27.8 |
   | 5 | 4.5 | 12 | 16 | | 13 | 8 | 24 | 32 |
   | 6 | 4.5 | 13 | 18 | | 14 | 9.8 | 25.8 | 33.5 |
   | 7 | 5.5 | 15 | 20 | | 15 | 9.2 | 26.5 | 34 |
   | 8 | 5.5 | 16.8 | 22.2 | | 16+ | ~10–12 | ~24–29 | ~33–40 |
   | 9 | 6.5 | 18 | 24.5 | | | | | |

5. **DC = CRISPR's `spellDC.moderate` column, exactly**, at every level 2–16 (sparse data above
   16 stays within ±1 except two outlier statblocks). RM's `getAbilityDCRange(level).high` is ~3
   too high.
6. **Ranged strikes become a separate 2-action volley** (73/162): "This volley is a
   `@Template[type:burst|distance:10]` within Y feet that deals `@Damage[...]` damage with a
   `@Check[reflex|dc:N|basic|options:area-effect]` save." Damage is **dice-only** (4d6, 6d8…),
   average ≈ the 2-action sweep line. Burst 10 (15 for long-range troops, range ≥ 120); range
   bands seen: 30/40/50/60/80/100/120/200. Recurring threshold hook: *"When the troop is reduced
   to 2 segments, this area decreases to a `@Template[type:burst|distance:Y−5]`."* No frequency.
7. **Stats are rebuilt at the troop's level keeping the base creature's shape**: the strong save
   stays strong, weak saves sometimes rise a band, nothing drops; speed/senses/languages/traits
   carry verbatim (+`troop`, size → Gargantuan, 157/162). Published HP normalizes to the
   **moderate** column for the troop level (median lands on it at every level; base HP ratio is
   meaningless, 0.8×–45×) — see decision 2 for our deliberate divergence.
8. **Signature abilities are re-skinned, never dropped** (Lich keeps its whole kit; Hell Hound's
   breath becomes twin cones with recharge). Caster troops keep a **real spellcasting entry**
   plus a "Troop Spellcasting" passive rider.
9. **Published area/splash weaknesses run higher than CRISPR's table, and area = splash.**
   Published medians step 5 (L3–6) → 8 (L7–8) → 10 (L9–12) → 12 (L13) → 15 (L14–18) → 20 (L19+),
   with splash equal to area; `TROOP_WEAKNESS_TABLE` sits 0–4 below with splash < area (L9: 8/6
   vs published 10/10; L14: 11/8 vs 15/15). Splash ≈ **half** area is specifically the **Form Up**
   cluster (5/2, 10/5, 15/8 — prior plan fact 4).
10. **The universal action kit is small.** Item-name frequency across all 162: Troop Defenses 162,
   Troop Movement 162, the 1-to-3 sweep 151, ranged volley 73, Form Up 34, Troop Spellcasting 11 —
   then a long tail under 10 (Mounted Troop 8, Drilled in Formations 7, Trample 4…). Everything
   common is in scope here; the tail is creature-specific flavor for the GM's manual pass.

Reference exemplars (all in `_pf2e-source/packs/pf2e/`): `battlecry-bestiary/goblin-rabble.json`
(L4, d6), `battlecry-bestiary/wolf-pack.json` (L6, d6 + trip rider),
`battlecry-bestiary/hobgoblin-veteran-regiment.json` (L9, d8 sweep + shortbow volley),
`battlecry-bestiary/viking-guard.json` (L11, d8), `pathfinder-monster-core-2/skeleton-infantry.json`
(10-ft reach emanation), base creatures in `pathfinder-monster-core/`.

## Settled decisions

1. **Only strikes transform (Mark).** Special abilities and spellcasting pass through untouched —
   the GM adapts them individually afterwards. No auto-adaptation of breath weapons etc.
2. **HP scales from its current value (Mark).** The creature's HP benchmark is preserved through
   the level bump — the benchmark system already recomputes stats at the new level, so this is
   *no code*: conversion must simply not touch benchmarks. Deliberate divergence from published
   practice (fact 7): a mook-origin troop runs leaner than its published equivalent; the editor's
   HP benchmark control is the fix-up. Document it.
3. **Names generate from the strike (Mark):** sweep → "`{Strike}` Flurry", volley → "`{Strike}`
   Volley" (matches published "Shortbow Volley"). Plain-English kernel defaults, overridable via
   options (i18n stays host-side; kernel stays vendorable); the GM renames freely.
4. **In-place conversion of the world copy (Mark).** Import-from-compendium already *is* the copy
   (fresh world id, `importedFrom` provenance); conversion differentiates it with the " Troop"
   name suffix + the `troop` trait. No reverse conversion — recovery = re-import the source.
   Export/packaging must not reuse the source compendium `_id` for a converted troop (see Wave 3).
5. **The recipe survives as overrides only** — `levelDelta`, `nameSuffix`, `defaultTroopSize`
   still honored; `generateAbilities` becomes *additive* extras merged by name. The sweep/volley/
   kit generation is CRISPR's default engine and runs recipe-less.
6. **RM coordination deferred (Mark handles later).** This is a breaking kernel change: bump the
   kernel tag as usual so RM can re-vendor (and delete most of `generateTroopAbilities`) whenever.

## Execution protocol (autonomous)

The orchestrating session runs each wave through this loop, committing between waves. Waves are
strictly sequential (each builds on the last); steps within the loop are sequential too.

```
for each wave W1..W4:
  1. wave-executor  (model per matrix)  — implements the wave per its spec below
  2. test-verifier  (default model)     — npm run check && npx vitest run; report triaged failures
  3. wave-executor  (same model)        — fix anything test-verifier found; re-verify
  4. wave-reviewer  (model per matrix)  — diff vs this doc's invariants + the wave's Done-when
  5. wave-executor fixes findings; re-review. Max 2 fix→review loops, then STOP (see below)
  6. commit (one commit per wave, message per the wave spec)
```

**Agent/model matrix** — Fable only where judgment is load-bearing; Opus where the spec pins the
work (Mark's directive):

| Wave | Executor | Reviewer | Rationale |
|------|----------|----------|-----------|
| W1 kernel generators + tables | **fable** | **fable** | Numeric-grammar fidelity: table smoothing, formula synthesis edge cases (d4 cap, secondary components, dice/mod solving). Highest blast radius; everything downstream consumes it. |
| W2 `applyTroopConversion` v2 | **opus** | **fable** | Rewrite against an explicit ordering spec — mechanical for the executor; the reviewer gate stays Fable because the change is breaking and idempotency is subtle. |
| W3 services/editor | **opus** | **opus** | Options plumbing + strings; risks are enumerated. `.svelte` edits go through the `svelte:svelte-file-editor` agent (or the executor loads the `svelte:svelte-core-bestpractices` skill) per repo tooling. |
| W4 acceptance + docs | **opus** | **opus** | Fixture tests transcribing numbers already pinned in this doc; docs. |

**Verification commands** (test-verifier runs these; nothing else counts as green):
`npm run check` and `npx vitest run`. The Playwright e2e suite is NOT part of the autonomous
loop — main has 4 known-failing specs unrelated to this work, and live-world verification is
Mark's manual step. W4's optional e2e spec is written but only smoke-run in isolation
(`npx playwright test <new-spec-file>`) if the harness boots cleanly; a harness failure is
reported, not fixed, and does not block the wave.

**Hard invariants the reviewer checks every wave:**
- `src/creature-builder/logic/` stays Foundry-free (no `game`, `foundry`, `fromUuid`, `Hooks`) —
  RM's vendor purity gate fails otherwise.
- v14 only, no v1 APIs; TypeScript everywhere; strings in `lang/en.json` for app-side UI (kernel
  English defaults are constants, host localizes).
- Comments only for non-obvious *why* (global CLAUDE.md).
- No behavior change to `applyTroopToActor`, `exportActorSource`, or the stat-snapshot export
  beyond what a wave's spec states.

**STOP conditions — end the run and report to Mark instead of pushing through:**
- A pinned published-number test can't be satisfied within tolerance without changing the
  tolerance or the table (means the grammar model is wrong somewhere — needs a human ruling).
- Kernel purity would be breached to make something work.
- Two fix→review loops on one wave without convergence.
- Anything requiring a decision this doc doesn't already settle.

Tag bump for the kernel (decision 6) happens once, after W2 lands, as part of that wave's commit.
No RM work anywhere in this plan.

## Wave 1 — kernel: damage table + generators

**Executor: fable. Reviewer: fable. Commit:** `Troop conversion v2 W1: troop action generators + damage/weakness tables`

**Files:** new `src/creature-builder/logic/troopActions.ts`; edit
`src/creature-builder/logic/creatureStatTables.ts` (weakness table only); new tests in
`src/creature-builder/tests/troopActions.test.ts`. Kernel only — no services, no UI.

**Steps:**
1. `TROOP_SWEEP_DAMAGE: Record<CreatureLevel, { one: number; two: number; three: number }>` —
   target averages for the three sweep lines. Author from the fact-4 medians, smoothed
   monotonically; extrapolate levels −1..1 and 17..24 on the same slope (≈ one: 3→13,
   two: 7→34, three: 10→46 across the full range). Judgment call reserved to the executor:
   exact smoothed values, provided every pinned test below passes.
2. `buildTroopSweep(strike: CreatureStrike, level: number, opts?: { name?: string }):
   CustomAbilityDefinition` — emits the fact-3 markup *exactly* (glyph header, Frequency once per
   round line, `<hr />`, Effect sentence, three glyph-numbered damage lines):
   - Area: `@Template[type:emanation|distance:5]`; distance 10 when the strike's traits include
     a `reach-N` entry with N ≥ 10.
   - Save: `@Check[reflex|dc:{spellDC.moderate for level}|basic|options:area-effect]`
     (via `getStatRangesForLevel(level).spellDC.moderate`).
   - Damage lines: keep the strike's die (parse `strike.customDamageFormula ?? strike.damage`
     with `parseDiceComponents`); dice counts N / 2N / 3N with the d4-cap rule (fact 4: cap at
     4 dice, grow only the mod); solve flat mods against `TROOP_SWEEP_DAMAGE[level]` via
     `adjustDamageFormulaToAverage`. Damage type from the strike. A persistent/secondary
     component becomes a second `@Damage` term scaling 1 die → 2 dice → 2 dice.
   - All damage tagged `|options:area-damage`.
   - Default name `"{strike.name} Flurry"`; `actionType: 'action'`, `actions: 1`, empty traits.
3. `buildTroopVolley(strike, level, opts?: { name?: string }): CustomAbilityDefinition` —
   2-action item, fact-6 wording exactly: burst 10 (15 when `strike.range ≥ 120`), "within Y
   feet" with Y = `strike.range` snapped to the nearest of {30,40,60,80,100,120,200} (default
   60 when unset), dice-only damage targeting `TROOP_SWEEP_DAMAGE[level].two` with the strike's
   die, same DC macro, closing sentence "When the troop is reduced to 2 segments, this area
   decreases to a `@Template[type:burst|distance:Y−5]`." Default name `"{strike.name} Volley"`.
4. Realign `TROOP_WEAKNESS_TABLE` to fact 9: area = splash, stepping 5 (L≤6, floor 2 below L2) →
   8 (L7–8) → 10 (L9–12) → 12 (L13) → 15 (L14–18) → 20 (L19+). Add
   `getTroopWeaknessValues(level, opts?: { formUp?: boolean })` (or a sibling) so Form Up seeds
   splash at **half** area. Seed-if-missing semantics in `withTroopWeaknesses` unchanged.
5. Tests (the pinned published numbers; tolerance ±2 on each line's average, DC exact,
   markup structure exact):
   - Goblin Rabble L4 d6 sweep → lines avg ≈ (3.5, 11, 14); published 1d6 / 2d6+4 / 2d6+7.
   - Wolf Pack L6 d6 sweep → ≈ (4.5, 14, 18); published 1d6+1 / 2d6+7 / 2d6+11.
   - Hobgoblin L9 d8 sweep → ≈ (6.5, 18, 24.5); published 1d8+2 / 2d8+9 / 3d8+11. DC 25.
   - Viking L11 d8 sweep → ≈ (7.5, 21, 28.5); published 1d8+3 / 2d8+12 / 3d8+15. DC 27.
   - Hobgoblin shortbow volley L9: burst 10, within 60, dice-only ≈ 14 (published 4d6), DC 25,
     shrink sentence present.
   - d4 cap: an L9+ d4 strike produces at most 4d4 with growing mods (wight pattern
     2d4 / 4d4+8 / 4d4+14).
   - Reach: a strike with `reach-10` trait → emanation 10.
   - Weakness table: L6 → 5/5, L9 → 10/10, L14 → 15/15; with formUp L9 → 10/5.

**Done when:** `npm run check` and `npx vitest run` green; every generator output contains
`options:area-damage`, `options:area-effect`, and a `@Template`; no Foundry imports in `logic/`.

## Wave 2 — kernel: `applyTroopConversion` v2 (breaking, tag bump)

**Executor: opus. Reviewer: fable. Commit:** `Troop conversion v2 W2: default conversion engine in applyTroopConversion (breaking)`

**Files:** `src/creature-builder/logic/troop.ts`, `src/creature-builder/logic/contracts.ts`
(options type only), `src/creature-builder/tests/troop.test.ts`. Kernel only.

**Steps — the default engine, in this order** (recipe fields override where noted):
1. Flag + size as today; `levelDelta` **defaults to 5** (recipe override; clamp −1..24;
   `rescaleCreatureIwr` unchanged). Name suffix defaults to `" Troop"`; **guard against
   re-applying a suffix already present**.
2. Post-bump, generate from the pre-conversion strikes: best melee strike (highest
   `calculateEffectiveDamage`-style average) → `buildTroopSweep`; best ranged strike
   (`isRanged`) → `buildTroopVolley`. Map through `customAbilityToSpecialAbility` (existing
   path, so `@Damage`/`@Check` parse into scalable values). Then **clear the converted strikes**
   (new `keepStrikes?: boolean` option retains them). No service work needed —
   `updateMeleeItems` already deletes strikes absent from the saved array.
3. Seed the standard kit as abilities with pure `@Localize` descriptions
   (`PF2E.NPC.Abilities.Glossary.TroopDefenses` / `TroopMovement`; `FormUp` behind a
   `formUp?: boolean` option, default false). Same content as `applyTroopToActor`'s glossary
   embeds so the editor and headless paths converge. No thresholds text — the system derives
   segments (prior plan fact 1). When `formUp` is set, the weakness seeding uses the half-splash
   variant (W1 step 4).
4. Merge `recipe.generateAbilities` extras (by-name dedup, unchanged) — now *additive* only.
5. Touch nothing else: benchmarks (decision 2), special abilities, spellcasting, senses, speeds,
   languages, non-area IWR pass through. Trait + area/splash weaknesses stay save-time
   (`troopAdjusted`), unchanged.
6. Options shape: extend the conversion entry points with
   `{ levelDelta?, troopSize?, formUp?, keepStrikes?, sweepName?, volleyName? }` — kernel-level,
   FE-free; `TroopConversionRecipe` keeps its existing fields (decision 5).
7. Tests: default recipe-less conversion of a wolf-like fixture (melee d6 strike) → level +5,
   " Troop" name, sweep present with W1 numbers, strikes empty, kit seeded, benchmarks/abilities
   untouched; ranged fixture → volley too; `keepStrikes: true` retains strikes; **idempotence:
   converting the result again changes nothing** (no double suffix, no duplicate kit, no new
   attacks); recipe overrides still win; `generateAbilities` extras merge additively.

**Done when:** `npm run check` + `npx vitest run` green; idempotence test passes; kernel purity
holds. This commit includes the kernel tag bump per repo convention (decision 6; no RM work).

## Non-goals

- **No signature-ability adaptation** (decision 1) — breath→cones, trip riders, aura re-skins are
  the GM's manual pass in the editor.
- **No caster-troop support** (Troop Spellcasting rider, spell area alterations) — spellcasting
  passes through untouched; follow-up if wanted.
- **No damage-type toggle rule elements** (skeleton/druid `RollOption`+`DamageAlteration` blocks).
- **No trample/Change Formation/self-buff templates** (fact 10 tail); no reverse conversion;
  **no RM changes** (decision 6).
